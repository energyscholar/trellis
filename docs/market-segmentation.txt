TRELLIS — Market Segmentation Analysis
Date: 2026-05-26
Source: External review of demo materials (Claude web evaluation)

================================================================
POSITIONING
================================================================

Trellis is not best understood as "an AI enhancement." It is closer
to "a professional cognitive operating environment."

Its adoption curve resembles version control, test-driven development,
secure coding practices, lab notebooks, or institutional QA processes:
at first annoying, niche, overengineered — then later indispensable
for serious work, especially once projects exceed the cognitive
capacity of single-session prompting.

================================================================
TARGET PROPERTIES
================================================================

Trellis governance is most valuable for work that is:

  - Long-horizon
  - High-context
  - Recursive
  - Error-sensitive
  - Artifact-heavy
  - Cognitively integrative

================================================================
TIER 1: VERY HIGH BENEFIT (~1-3% of users)
================================================================

Users who experience a dramatic capability jump.

  - Independent researchers
  - Systems thinkers
  - Technical founders
  - Scientific synthesists
  - Advanced software architects
  - Institutional strategy people
  - Technical writers producing book-length work
  - Complex investigative analysts
  - R&D groups
  - Governance/safety researchers
  - People building long-running conceptual frameworks

These users suffer enormously from drift, forgotten corrections,
context collapse, inconsistent terminology, hallucinated reframing,
and loss of epistemic continuity. Trellis directly attacks those
failure modes.

================================================================
TIER 2: MODERATE BENEFIT (~5-15% of users)
================================================================

Users who gain meaningfully but may not fully exploit the system.

  - Lawyers
  - Compliance-heavy business users
  - Consultants
  - Enterprise analysts
  - Product managers
  - Telehealth/sales ops
  - Grant writers
  - Academic labs
  - Policy teams
  - Investigative journalists

They need continuity, disciplined outputs, auditability, persistent
preferences, and controlled workflows. They may not care about
ACS/topology at all. They experience Trellis as: "an AI that behaves
professionally and remembers process." That alone has value.

================================================================
TIER 3: LOW BENEFIT (~40-60% of users)
================================================================

Users who gain only incremental improvements.

  - Ordinary coding assistants
  - Casual writing
  - Brainstorming
  - Student homework
  - Consumer chatbot usage
  - Quick Q&A

Their sessions are short, low-context, disposable, and not
continuity-sensitive. The governance overhead may outweigh benefits.

================================================================
TIER 4: NEGATIVE/RESISTED (~20-40% of users)
================================================================

Users who would actively dislike it.

  - Users who want maximal AI agreeableness
  - People who prefer improvisational prompting
  - Rapid-fire "vibe coding" workflows
  - Users who dislike friction or governance
  - People who optimize for novelty over stability

Trellis deliberately imposes discipline, boundary maintenance,
correction persistence, governance transparency, and reduced impulsive
generation. Some users will experience that as "less creative" or
"less fun" — even if operational reliability improves.

================================================================
END OF ANALYSIS
