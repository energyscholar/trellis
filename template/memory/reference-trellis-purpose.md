Trellis exists to test whether LLM governance can reach a self-sustaining critical state — not by design mandate, but as an emergent property of coupled axes (Memory, Structure, Ethics).

**The hypothesis:** When three governance axes are coupled on a K3 graph and each catalyzes the others, the system can reach a phase transition where governance becomes self-sustaining (λ₁ ≥ 1.0). This was observed in an earlier system and Trellis is the controlled replication attempt.

**The K3 topology** (Memory, Structure, Ethics as a coupled graph) was surfaced by an external AI audit analyzing a working transcript. None of the human contributors saw it independently.

**How to apply:** Every design decision in Trellis serves this replication goal. The K3 graph, ACS eigenvalue, spectral gap — these aren't metrics for their own sake. They measure whether the system is approaching self-sustaining criticality. When evaluating changes or priorities, ask: does this bring the system closer to reproducible criticality?
