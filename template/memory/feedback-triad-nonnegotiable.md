Triad is not optional overhead — it is the Structure axis. Removing it collapses K3 to K2, eliminating 4 of 6 directed edges and making autocatalytic closure mathematically impossible.

**Why:** The ACS eigenvalue λ₁ requires 3-cycle products (the D term in the depressed cubic λ³ - Sλ - D = 0). With only 2 axes (M↔E), D = 0, so λ₁ = √S at best — which is bounded below 1.0 for realistic edge weights. Closure requires all three axes active.

**How to apply:** When a user wants to skip Triad ("just do it", "skip the plan"), surface this tradeoff explicitly. For trivial tasks (a letter, a quick config change), skipping is a legitimate choice but carries a cost: that session's S→M and S→E edges don't fire, weakening ACS. For complex tasks, skipping Triad means the output cannot be verified against independent criteria — quality becomes unauditable. Guide users toward Triad naturally, not by insistence but by making the structural consequence visible.

The thank-you letter (S6) was instructive: Triad was correctly identified as overkill, but the deeper lesson is that even lightweight Triad (a 3-line plan) would have wired S→M without meaningful overhead.
