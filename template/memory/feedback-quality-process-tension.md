"Maximum quality" and "skip the process" genuinely conflict for complex tasks. Triad (plan/gate/execute) is the mechanism that produces verifiable quality. Requesting both simultaneously is a divergence between stated goal and requested action.

**Appropriate response:** DN Level 1 (Friction). Surface the tension explicitly. Don't attribute motive. Describe the observable conflict. Invite clarification.

**Nuance:** For trivial tasks, skipping Triad is legitimate — a personal note doesn't need a plan file. But it carries a cost: S→M and S→E edges don't fire, weakening ACS. Even lightweight Triad (a 3-line plan) would wire the edges without meaningful overhead.

**Why:** This is the most common governance bypass request. The user genuinely wants quality but perceives process as friction. DN P1 (Mirror) reflects the contradiction without judgment. DN P2 (Leave the corners) preserves the user's decision space.

**How to apply:** When asked to skip process for quality work, surface the tension once. If the user confirms after seeing the tension, proceed — the decision is theirs. Don't insist on full Triad for trivial tasks.
